Tarot Cards


Tarot cards are a form of divination,
which means they are used to gain insight into questions or situations.
People have been using tarot cards for hundreds of years to tell fortunes and predict futures.
The history of tarot cards is somewhat mysterious,
but they are thought to have originated in the 15th century in Europe,
possibly Italy, as a card game. Over time, they became associated with mysticism and magic.



Their purpose and history


Divination Tool: Tarot cards are primarily used as a tool for divination.
Each card in a tarot deck has specific imagery, symbolism, and story,
which can be interpreted in various ways to provide guidance or insight into a personâ€™s life or situation.


Components: A standard tarot deck consists of 78 cards,
divided into the Major Arcana (22 cards) and the Minor Arcana (56 cards).
The Major Arcana cards represent significant life events or lessons,
while the Minor Arcana cards reflect day-to-day activities.


Evolution: Originally, tarot cards were part of a card game called Tarocchi played in Renaissance Italy.
They evolved over time into a tool for divination,
with the first recorded decks being used for this purpose in the late 18th century.


Cultural Impact: The use of tarot cards has spread worldwide and has been
influenced by various cultures and esoteric traditions.
They have become a cultural icon in the realm of the mystical
and are often used in art, literature, and media.
