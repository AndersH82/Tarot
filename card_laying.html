Simple tarot readings for beginners


As promised, I'll start with a number of simple tarot readings for beginners.
Although these lays are a little easier, they may also be worth repeating if you already have some experience.

Single card laying

This is a simple tarot spread that only involves a single card.
The purpose of a one-card layout is to get quick and easy guidance on a specific question or situation.



Do this

1.Shuffle the cards while focusing on your question.
2.Draw a card and place it in front of you.
3.Interpret the card based on your question.

To find out the meanings of the tarot cards,
you can click on the link and read my interpretations of all the cards in the tarot deck.

Three card laying


This is another simple tarot spread that involves three different cards.
The purpose of this orientation is to get an overview of a situation.
The three positions can have different meanings, but here I focus on the most popular variant.
The three positions then represent the past (past), present and future.


Do this

1.Shuffle and draw three cards while focusing on your question.
2.Place the cards in a row in front of you, from left to right.
3.Interpret the cards with your question in mind.

When making your interpretation, you should consider the respective positions of the cards:

First card (past tense). The first card represents the past associated with your question or situation.
Second card (present). The second card symbolizes the present, that is, the present situation or the present influences.
Third card (future). The third card gives an indication of how things may develop in the future.

First, analyze each card individually. If you should interpret the cards, you can take a look in a guide book.
You can also find the traditional meanings of the cards on the linked page. Then think about how the different cards affect each other.
Can you see a connection and a development or story that extends from the first card to the third card?


Five card laying

This is another fairly simple tarot spread that involves five cards.
Its purpose is to gain a more detailed insight into a complex situation or issue.


Do this

1.Shuffle and draw five cards while focusing on your question.
2.Place the cards in a cross or star shape.
3.Interpret the cards.

When you interpret the cards,
you can assume that the different positions in the layout have the following meanings:

First card (current situation). This card provides an overview of the current situation or current problem.
Second card (possible obstacles). This card represents obstacles or challenges that you may face.
Third card (past influences). This card focuses on the past and how it has affected the present situation.
Fourth card (recommended action). This card provides advice or recommends an action that may be helpful.
Fifth card (possible outcome). This card indicates a possible future or outcome based on where it is headed.

First analyze each card individually.
If you need to, you can consult a guidebook or read about the meanings of the tarot cards online.

When making your interpretation, you should look at the big picture and try to understand how the cards relate to each other.
Do you see a natural progression or are there contrasts that need to be explored?

Summary of these simple tarot readings for beginners

By starting with these simple tarot spreads, you get a good introduction to the basics of tarot reading.
These lays are easy to do and require no previous experience.
They also help you build your ability to interpret and understand how the cards can interact with each other in a layout.
Once you've become comfortable with these basic lays, you can move on to the more complex variations below.

Medium complex tarot readings for you with some experience

Once you've familiarized yourself with the basic tarot spreads above, it's time to move on to slightly more complex spreads.
The variants that I present below can offer slightly deeper and more nuanced insights.
This part of the guide is aimed at you with a certain understanding of tarot who want to expand your knowledge and deepen your interpretations.

Celtic cross

The Celtic cross is a slightly more advanced tarot spread than the ones above.
The purpose of this tarot reading is to provide a comprehensive overview of a situation.
It provides insight into both underlying factors, possible challenges and future development.
