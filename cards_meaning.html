What do the tarot cards really mean?


The tarot deck contains 78 cards with unique imagery, meanings and stories to tell:
The 22 cards in the Major Arcana represent the different archetypes and stages of life.
These cards are often used in tarot readings to provide an overview and insight into larger life events and deeper emotional or spiritual issues.
The 56 cards in the Minor Arcana are divided into four suits: the suit of cups, the suit of swords, the suit of coins and the suit of wands.
The various cards in this part of the tarot deck often deal with more everyday events and feelings and offer concrete advice.
Some claim that the tarot cards are just a collection of plastic cards with different images.
However, having used and interpreted the cards for many years, I have learned that this is not the case.
Instead, I see the tarot deck as a kind of storybook of life. It reflects the soul and gives us access to our inner wisdom.
A good examples of this are the journey of the fool in the Major Arcana and the progression in the various suites in the Minor Arcana.
