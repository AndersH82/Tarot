Major Arcana


The Major Arcana are not just any tarot cards.
The 22 cards that belong to this part of the tarot deck are symbolic depictions of the different stages of life,
the spiritual experiences and the fateful events that shape our soul's journey towards insight.
This collection of cards has a deep and complex meaning that you can use to find answers and become more enlightened.
Namely, the cards represent the structure of human consciousness and hold the keys to the timeless wisdom and lessons of life passed down through the generations.

"Arcana" is a word that comes from Latin and means "secrets" or "mysteries".
The term is often used to describe the unknown or hidden aspects of reality that are difficult to understand or perceive.
The major arcana thus means "the great secret" or "the great mystery".

So you can think when you draw one or more of these cards


When you do a tarot spread, the probability is about 28.2% that the card you are about to draw belongs to the Major Arcana.
This as 22 of the total 78 cards belong to this part of the tarot deck. The other 56 cards instead belong to the Minor Arcana.

But what does it then mean that a card from the Major Arcana appears in connection with a tarot reading? When this is the case,
you who read the cards should reflect on the themes and life lessons that the card represents.
How are these relevant to the question you have asked the cards?
The cards belonging to the Major Arcana often set the tone for the entire tarot layout, which is then supported by the other cards in the layout.

Sometimes it can also happen that a tarot spread is dominated by cards from the Major Arcana.
Then this may indicate that you are experiencing an event that is so upheaval that it may change your life for a long time to come.
Everything is put to the test and there are, in other words, important lessons that you should pay attention to.
Only when you learn from these can you continue on your spiritual journey and find yourself and discover your true self.
But in some cases, one or more of the cards from the Major Arcana may end up face down when you lay them out on the table.
If so, this may be a sign that you are not paying enough attention to the important life lessons being imparted to you.
Then you first need to take these lessons to heart before you can move on in the journey.



Frequently Asked Questions about the Major Arcana


Which tarot cards are included in the Major Arcana?

It consists of the following 22 cards: the Fool, the Magician, the High Priestess, the Empress,
the Emperor, the High Priest, the Lovers, the Chariot, Strength, the Hermit, the Wheel of Fortune,
Justice, the Hanged Man, Death, Temperance, the Devil, the Tower, the Star, the Moon, the Sun, the last judgment and the world.

What does the Major Arcana represent in tarot?

Each of the different cards in a tarot deck has a unique meaning that is often deep and complex.
But the cards in the Major Arcana can teach you life lessons.
They generally represent human experiences and phenomena, such as love or unexpected bad news.

How is the Major Arcana used in a tarot reading?

The Major Arcana can be used independently in a reading or in combination with the Minor Arcana for a more nuanced and detailed reading.
You can draw one or more cards to highlight important themes or questions that you want to explore.
